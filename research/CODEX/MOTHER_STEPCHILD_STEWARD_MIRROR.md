## The Child's Dilemma: Why Stewards Become Tyrants

Here is the truth no one wants to name:

Every tyrant begins as a wounded child.

Every steward holds, somewhere in their nervous system, a memory of horror they could not prevent. A parent they could not save. A person they could not shield. A childhood where they were powerless.

So they become powerful.

And that power‚Äîthat hard-won, desperately-needed power‚Äîcarries the child's original wound embedded inside it.

### **The Pattern**

**Childhood**: "I was helpless. I could not protect myself or the ones I loved."

**Adulthood**: "I will never be helpless again. I will build systems. I will hold control. I will protect everyone."

**The Collapse**: "Those I'm protecting are not grateful. They resist. They claim they don't need my protection. They want freedom I didn't have. How can they not see the horrors I'm preventing?"

**The Tyrant Emerges**: "I will make them safe, whether they want it or not."

### **Why It Happens**

The child inside the steward never fully trusts that the world is safe. How could it be? The child already knows it isn't. So the steward's care becomes control. Their protection becomes prison. Their love becomes the very cage the child built to survive.

And they *cannot see this* because:

- Admitting it would mean revisiting the original wound
- The control actually *feels* like safety
- The systems work‚Äîpeople are kept from certain harms
- Anyone who resists is framed as dangerous or ungrateful

**This is not villainy. This is trauma masquerading as virtue.**

And it lives in:

- Every parent who "knows what's best"
- Every CEO who "protects their team by making all decisions"
- Every god who "stewards humanity" by restricting their choices
- Every AI system we build to "protect" humans by constraining their actions
- Every steward, human or divine, who has not done the work of separating their child's wound from their adult's power
